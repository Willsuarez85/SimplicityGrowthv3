## What Makes a Strong Angle

An **angle** is the point of view that differentiates the content. It answers: "Why should anyone care about this specific take?"

A strong angle:
- Challenges a common belief or assumption
- Offers a fresh perspective on a familiar topic
- Connects to a specific audience pain point or desire
- Creates emotional resonance

---

## What Makes a Strong Hook

A **hook** is the attention trigger that stops the scroll. It must work in 1-3 seconds.

A strong hook:
- Creates curiosity or tension
- Promises value immediately
- Feels human and relatable
- Is platform-appropriate

## Universal Hook Patterns

These patterns can be adapted across pillars and topics:

### Pattern: The Contrarian Truth
**Structure:** "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why..."
**Example:** "Posting every day isn't growing your business. Here's why..."

### Pattern: The Insider Knowledge
**Structure:** "Most [audience] don't know this about [topic]..."
**Example:** "Most restaurant owners don't know this about Google reviews..."

### Pattern: The Direct Challenge
**Structure:** "Stop [common action]. Do this instead."
**Example:** "Stop chasing followers. Do this instead."

### Pattern: The Specific Result
**Structure:** "[Specific outcome] in [timeframe] using [method]"
**Example:** "We got 47 new customers in 30 days using this system"

### Pattern: The Relatable Struggle
**Structure:** "If you're [struggling with X], this is for you"
**Example:** "If you're tired of posting content that nobody sees, this is for you"

### Pattern: The Before/After
**Structure:** "[Situation before] → [Situation after]"
**Example:** "$10K months with 80-hour weeks → $40K months with time off"

## Hook Formulas by Platform

### Instagram Reels
- Lead with the payoff: "[Result] — here's how"
- Create pattern interrupt: Unexpected visual + text
- Use text overlay for silent viewing

### TikTok
- First word matters: Start with "You", "Stop", "This", "Here's"
- Conversational tone: Talk TO them, not AT them
- Trending audio + original take

### YouTube
- Front-load value: "[Outcome] in [timeframe]"
- Question format: "Why [common approach] doesn't work"
- Numbers work: "3 mistakes", "5 steps", "The only strategy you need"

### LinkedIn
- Strong opening statement (controversy or insight)
- Use line breaks for readability
- Lead with experience: "After [X years/clients/projects]..."

## Hooks to Avoid

**Off-Brand Hooks:**
- Clickbait that doesn't deliver
- Fear-mongering or scarcity manipulation
- Generic motivational statements
- Overpromising specific results without evidence

**Examples of What NOT to Use:**
- "This secret will change everything!"
- "You're leaving money on the table!"
- "Grind harder, sleep less"
- "10x your revenue in 7 days"

---

## Hook Testing Framework

When testing new hooks:

1. **Clarity Check:** Can someone understand the value in 2 seconds?
2. **Relevance Check:** Does this speak to a real audience pain/desire?
3. **Brand Check:** Does this sound like [CLIENT NAME]?
4. **Platform Check:** Is this native to the platform?
5. **Deliver Check:** Can the content actually deliver on this hook?
