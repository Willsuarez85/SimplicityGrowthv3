# Ifixx Visual Identity Guide

**Client:** Ifixx Services
**Location:** Charlotte, NC
**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Brand Visual Essence

Ifixx's visual identity communicates **clarity, urgency, trust, and professionalism**. The design system supports the brand positioning as "Charlotte's Emergency Handyman" - fast, honest, and local.

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity First** - Every element should communicate instantly
2. **Action-Oriented** - Visuals should drive response
3. **Professional Simplicity** - Clean, uncluttered, trustworthy
4. **Urgency When Needed** - Red accents for emergency messaging
5. **Warmth & Approachability** - Human, not corporate

## Color Palette

### Primary Colors

| Color | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|-------|-----|-----|-------|
| **White** | `#ffffff` | 255, 255, 255 | Primary backgrounds, clean space |
| **Yellow** | `#ffd600` | 255, 214, 0 | CTAs, action buttons, attention |
| **Red** | `#e53e3e` | 229, 62, 62 | Emergency messaging, urgency, alerts |

### Secondary Colors

| Color | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|-------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Light Gray** | `#f9fafb` | 249, 250, 251 | Secondary sections, subtle backgrounds |
| **Dark Gray** | `#1f2937` | 31, 41, 55 | Text, headings, structure |

### Color Psychology

- **White**: Cleanliness, professionalism, trust, fresh start
- **Yellow**: Energy, action, optimism, visibility, attention-grabbing
- **Red**: Urgency, emergency, importance, immediate action needed
- **Light Gray**: Calm, neutral, professional backdrop
- **Dark Gray**: Authority, stability, readability, grounding

### Color Ratios

```
Standard Content:
├── White: 60% (backgrounds, space)
├── Dark Gray: 25% (text, structure)
├── Yellow: 10% (CTAs, highlights)
└── Light Gray: 5% (sections)

Emergency Content:
├── White: 50% (backgrounds)
├── Red: 25% (urgency elements)
├── Dark Gray: 15% (text)
└── Yellow: 10% (CTAs)
```

### Color Application Rules

**DO:**
- Use yellow for primary CTAs ("Call Now", "Book Online", "Get Quote")
- Use red sparingly for genuine emergency/urgency messaging
- Maintain high contrast for readability
- Use white space generously for clarity

**DON'T:**
- Use red for non-emergency content (dilutes urgency)
- Combine yellow and red in close proximity (visual clash)
- Use dark backgrounds as primary (reduces trustworthiness)
- Overuse yellow (loses impact)

## Typography

### Font Strategy

**Primary:** Sans-serif fonts for maximum readability and professional appearance.

### Recommended Font Families

| Use Case | Font | Fallback |
|----------|------|----------|
| Headings | Inter Bold | Helvetica Neue, Arial |
| Subheadings | Inter Semi-Bold | Helvetica Neue, Arial |
| Body | Inter Regular | Helvetica Neue, Arial |
| CTAs | Inter Bold | Helvetica Neue, Arial |

### Type Scale

```
H1 - Hero Headlines: 48-64px / Bold
H2 - Section Headers: 32-40px / Semi-Bold
H3 - Subsection Titles: 24-28px / Semi-Bold
H4 - Card Headers: 20-24px / Medium
Body - Content: 16-18px / Regular
Small - Captions: 14px / Regular
```

### Typography Principles

1. **Readability**: All text must be instantly scannable
2. **Hierarchy**: Clear visual distinction between levels
3. **Accessibility**: Minimum 16px body text, high contrast
4. **Consistency**: Same fonts across all touchpoints

## Logo Usage

### Primary Logo

The Ifixx logo should:
- Be displayed prominently on all materials
- Maintain minimum clear space around it
- Never be distorted, rotated, or modified
- Appear on white or light backgrounds when possible

### Logo Variations

| Variation | Usage |
|-----------|-------|
| Full Color | Primary use on white/light backgrounds |
| Dark Mono | On yellow or light colored backgrounds |
| White/Reverse | On dark gray or photo backgrounds |

### Minimum Sizes

- **Digital**: 120px width minimum
- **Print**: 1 inch width minimum

### Clear Space

Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "i" in "ifixx" on all sides.

## Photography Style

### Aesthetic Guidelines

**Subjects:**
- Real work in progress (hands-on, authentic)
- Charlotte homes and neighborhoods
- The team (Jaime, Hamed) in action
- Before/after transformations
- Close-ups of quality craftsmanship

**Style:**
- Natural lighting preferred
- Clean, uncluttered compositions
- Action shots over posed portraits
- Show the work, not just the workers
- Authentic, not stock-photo feeling

### Photo Categories

| Category | Purpose | Examples |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Action Shots | Show expertise | Repairing leak, installing fixture |
| Before/After | Demonstrate results | Kitchen transformation, drywall repair |
| Team | Build trust | Owners at work, team collaboration |
| Neighborhoods | Establish locality | Charlotte homes, recognizable areas |

### Photo Treatment

- Maintain natural colors (avoid heavy filters)
- Ensure proper lighting (no dark, shadowy images)
- Include yellow brand elements when possible (truck, shirt, tools)
- Caption with service type and location when relevant

## Brand Application Examples

### Emergency Messaging

When promoting emergency services:
- Use red as accent (25% of composition)
- Bold, urgent language
- Phone number extremely prominent
- "24/7" or "Same Day" clearly visible

### Standard Handyman Messaging

For regular handyman services:
- Yellow accents for CTAs
- Show before/after or in-progress work
- List specific services
- Emphasize reliability and trust

### Transformation/Remodeling Messaging

For larger projects:
- High-quality portfolio photos
- Yellow accents minimal
- Emphasize "5 Years" and "100+ Projects"
- More aspirational, less urgent tone
