## Executive Summary

This document analyzes repeating content patterns across top-performing AI education creators. The analysis identifies format structures, hook types, video lengths, and production approaches that dominate the market.

---

## Video Structure Patterns

### Pattern 1: Tutorial Build-Along

**Structure:**
1. Hook (problem statement) - 0:00-0:30
2. What we're building (outcome preview) - 0:30-1:30
3. Requirements/setup - 1:30-3:00
4. Step-by-step build - 3:00-[main content]
5. Demo/test - [near end]
6. Next steps/CTA - Final 30-60 seconds

**Used By:** David Ondrej, Igor Pogany, Victor Perez IA

**Example Title Patterns:**
- "How to Build [X] with [Tool] in [Time]"
- "Create Your Own [X] Using [Tool] - Step by Step"
- "[Tool] Tutorial: Build [X] from Scratch"

---

### Pattern 2: News Roundup

**Structure:**
1. Cold open with biggest story - 0:00-0:15
2. Intro/branding - 0:15-0:30
3. Story 1 (biggest) - 0:30-5:00
4. Story 2-4 (supporting) - 5:00-15:00
5. Opinions/implications - 15:00-18:00
6. CTA/outro - Final minute

**Used By:** Matt Wolfe, Wes Roth, DotCSV

**Example Title Patterns:**
- "AI News This Week: [Biggest Story]"
- "[Company] Just Changed Everything"
- "The AI News You Missed This Week"

---

### Pattern 3: Explainer/Deep Dive

**Structure:**
1. Hook question - 0:00-0:30
2. Context/background - 0:30-3:00
3. Core concept explanation - 3:00-10:00
4. Implications/applications - 10:00-15:00
5. Future outlook - 15:00-18:00
6. Summary/CTA - Final minute

**Used By:** DotCSV, Wes Roth

**Example Title Patterns:**
- "What is [Concept] and Why Does It Matter?"
- "[Concept] Explained: Everything You Need to Know"
- "The Truth About [Topic]"

---

### Pattern 4: Agency/Business Model

**Structure:**
1. Results hook (income/clients) - 0:00-0:30
2. Business model overview - 0:30-3:00
3. Step-by-step breakdown - 3:00-15:00
4. Tools/resources needed - 15:00-20:00
5. Common mistakes - 20:00-25:00
6. CTA to community/course - Final 2 minutes

**Used By:** Liam Ottley

**Example Title Patterns:**
- "How I Built a $[X] AI Agency in [Time]"
- "The AI Business Model Nobody is Talking About"
- "Start an AI Automation Agency in 2025"

## Hook Types Analysis

### Highest-Performing Hook Categories

#### 1. Problem-Agitate Hook
**Formula:** State problem -> Show consequences -> Hint at solution

**Examples:**
- "Most people are using ChatGPT completely wrong..."
- "You're wasting hours on tasks AI could do in seconds..."
- "This one mistake is costing you clients..."

**Usage:** Victor Perez IA, Liam Ottley

---

#### 2. Result-First Hook
**Formula:** Show outcome -> Create curiosity -> Promise to teach

**Examples:**
- "This AI agent just saved me 20 hours of work..."
- "I built a complete system in 10 minutes with Claude Code..."
- "This one automation generates $5K/month..."

**Usage:** Liam Ottley, David Ondrej

---

#### 3. News/Urgency Hook
**Formula:** Breaking news -> Why it matters -> What you'll learn

**Examples:**
- "OpenAI just released something huge..."
- "Anthropic changed everything with this update..."
- "This AI tool dropped yesterday and it's insane..."

**Usage:** Matt Wolfe, Wes Roth, DotCSV

---

#### 4. Contrarian Hook
**Formula:** Challenge assumption -> Present alternative -> Prove it

**Examples:**
- "Forget everything you know about prompting..."
- "AI influencers won't tell you this..."
- "This free tool beats $99/month alternatives..."

**Usage:** Greg Isenberg, Wes Roth

---

#### 5. Question Hook
**Formula:** Ask compelling question -> Tease answer -> Build anticipation

**Examples:**
- "What if you could automate your entire business?"
- "Is Claude Code actually worth learning?"
- "Can AI really replace developers?"

**Usage:** DotCSV, Igor Pogany

## Format Evolution Observations

### What's Decreasing (2024-2025)

1. **Pure news commentary** - Oversaturated
2. **Tool reviews without tutorials** - Low value perception
3. **"Make money with AI" clickbait** - Audience fatigue
4. **Short, surface-level content** - Demand for depth

### What's Increasing (2025)

1. **Build-along tutorials** - Hands-on learning demand
2. **System/workflow builds** - Complete solutions over tools
3. **Long-form deep dives** - Quality over quantity
4. **Live coding/building** - Authenticity and proof
5. **Community-integrated content** - Engagement beyond video

## Spanish Market Specific Patterns

### What Spanish Creators Do

1. **Academic/explanatory tone** - More educational, less hype
2. **Longer intros** - More context setting
3. **Less aggressive CTAs** - Softer promotion
4. **Spain-centric references** - European examples/context

### What's Missing in Spanish

1. **Build-along tutorials for agents** - Almost nonexistent
2. **Business application focus** - Mostly conceptual
3. **Claude Code content** - Zero meaningful presence
4. **MCP tutorials** - Zero content identified
5. **Vibe coding content** - Emerging but minimal

## Pattern Summary

### Most Effective Patterns (Based on Engagement)

1. **Tutorial + specific outcome** - "Build X with Y"
2. **Long-form deep dives** - 20-40 minutes for complex topics
3. **Screen recording + face cam** - Personal connection
4. **Problem-first hooks** - Immediate relevance
5. **Demo at end** - Proof of outcome

### Patterns to Avoid (Showing Fatigue)

1. News-only commentary without application
2. "Make $X with AI" promises
3. Tool reviews without tutorials
4. Surface-level "tips and tricks"
5. Overly academic without practical examples
