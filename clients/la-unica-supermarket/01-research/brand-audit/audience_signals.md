## 3. Customer Segments Identified

### Primary Customer Types (Based on Review Evidence)

**1. Hispanic/Latino Immigrants**
- Seeking authentic products from home countries
- Example: Customer from Honduras finding childhood favorites
- Looking for products not available in mainstream stores
- Language: Spanish preferred, bilingual

**2. Cultural Food Explorers**
- Non-Hispanic customers seeking authentic Latin food
- Appreciate "Not Tex-Mex" distinction
- Value the diner/restaurant experience
- Looking for genuine cultural experience

**3. Budget-Conscious Shoppers**
- Seeking competitive prices on fresh meat
- Value variety and quality for price
- Compare to other stores (CompareFood mentioned)

**4. Fresh Meat/Seafood Seekers**
- Specifically visiting for butcher/carniceria
- Value freshly cut options
- Appreciate well-stocked meat counter
- Fish market customers

**5. Convenience Shoppers**
- Using multiple services (grocery + money transfers)
- Dining at in-store restaurant
- One-stop shopping appeal

## 10. Key Customer Insights

### What Customers Value Most
1. **Fresh meat** - The carniceria is the star attraction
2. **Authenticity** - Real Latin American products, not Americanized versions
3. **Cleanliness** - Store appearance matters
4. **Cultural connection** - Finding products from home
5. **Convenience** - Multiple services in one location

### What Would Make Customers Happier
1. Consistent produce freshness
2. More consistent service quality
3. Competitive pricing perception
4. More engagement/response to feedback

### What Drives Loyalty
- Finding rare products from home country
- Fresh meat quality
- Cultural atmosphere and connection
- Restaurant convenience
- Competitive alternative to Compare Foods
